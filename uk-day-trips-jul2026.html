Sat 11 Jul · sunny, 28°
Shugborough Estate + Lichfield
⏱ ~15–20 min each way

Gentle opener for the trips. Morning at the National Trust estate — walled garden, riverside parkland, farm animals, all pram-friendly. Then Lichfield's three-spired medieval cathedral and Beacon Park playground. Shortest drive on the list, so easiest to cut short if Alfie's tired after the BBQ day. Dry & sunny — good weekend crowds tolerance on a big estate.

Sun 12 Jul · sunny & dry, 27°
Ironbridge Gorge + Blists Hill
⏱ ~35 min each way · ~55 mi round

The forecast flipped — Sun 12 is now bone dry & sunny (<5% rain), so the open-air Blists Hill goes ahead as planned rather than a rain day. UNESCO World Heritage: start at Blists Hill Victorian Town, a full recreated town with costumed characters and working shops; wide flat lanes make it one of the most pram-friendly attractions anywhere. Then the Iron Bridge itself and a riverside café lunch. (Wet-day backup, just in case: Enginuity indoor museum on site.)

Conwy Castle · Wikimedia Commons
Mon 13 Jul · sunny, 25°, breezy
North Wales — Conwy + Llandudno
⏱ ~1 hr 45 each way (the big one)

A proper adventure day, saved for a quiet weekday. Forecast is ideal — sunny, ~25°C with a fresh sea breeze (gusts to ~27mph, so a windproof layer on the prom). Conwy is a spectacular walled town with one of Europe's best-preserved castles right on the estuary; then 10 min to Llandudno's grand Victorian seaside promenade (flat, pram-perfect) and the Great Orme tram for big views. Set off early.

Chatsworth House, Peak District · Wikimedia Commons
Tue 14 Jul · warm 28°, PM cloud
Chatsworth House + Bakewell
⏱ ~1 hr each way

The showstopper. One of England's grandest stately homes in glorious parkland. Do the farmyard, adventure playground and river walk (all pram-friendly) rather than the full house if Alfie's restless — the grounds alone are worth it. Then 15 min to Bakewell for the classic Peak market town and a warm Bakewell pudding. Big walking day; a slight chance of afternoon cloud/showers (~30%), and the house is the perfect rain-resilient fallback if it turns.

Chester Zoo · Wikimedia Commons
Wed 15 Jul · warm 28°, PM showers
Chester Zoo or Shrewsbury
⏱ Zoo ~1 hr · Shrewsbury ~45 min

Pick your energy level. Chester Zoo is world-class, totally pram-built and an easy full day — and its big indoor houses (Islands, tropical) make it the right pick if the ~30% afternoon showers arrive. Or keep it relaxed with Shrewsbury — the prettiest medieval town nearby, Tudor streets wrapped in a loop of the Severn, plus the big pram-friendly Quarry Park by the river.

Cotswolds — Bourton-on-the-Water or rest

Optional last outing, or keep it as slack and packing before the London leg. If it's wet, an indoor day (Birmingham Thinktank / soft-play) or just pack for London.
