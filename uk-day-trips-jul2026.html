Shugborough Hall · Wikimedia Commons
Fri 10 Jul · hot
Shugborough Estate + Lichfield
⏱ ~15–20 min each way

Gentle opener. Morning at the National Trust estate — walled garden, riverside parkland, farm animals, all pram-friendly. Then Lichfield's three-spired medieval cathedral and Beacon Park playground. Shortest drive on the list, so easiest to cut short if the heat's hard on Alfie.

Blists Hill Victorian Town, Ironbridge · Wikimedia Commons
Sat 11 Jul · dry & warm
Ironbridge Gorge + Blists Hill
⏱ ~35 min each way · ~55 mi round

Moved here from Sun 12 to dodge Sunday's rain — Blists Hill is largely open-air, so it wants the dry day. UNESCO World Heritage: start at Blists Hill Victorian Town, a full recreated town with costumed characters and working shops; wide flat lanes make it one of the most pram-friendly attractions anywhere. Then the Iron Bridge itself and a riverside café lunch.

Sun 12 Jul · rain — rest / indoor
Rest day (week's wettest)
⏱ on the doorstep

Forecast flags Sun 12 as the week's wettest (~9mm, moderate wind), so this is now the deliberate breather — an early Cannock Chase stroll if it's dry first thing, otherwise relax at your dad's. If you want out, keep it indoor: Enginuity hands-on museum at Ironbridge, a soft-play, or Birmingham's Thinktank science museum (~40 min).

Conwy Castle · Wikimedia Commons
Mon 13 Jul · cooler, weekday
North Wales — Conwy + Llandudno
⏱ ~1 hr 45 each way (the big one)

A proper adventure day, saved for a quiet weekday and cooler air. Conwy is a spectacular walled town with one of Europe's best-preserved castles right on the estuary; then 10 min to Llandudno's grand Victorian seaside promenade (flat, pram-perfect) and the Great Orme tram for big views. Set off early.

Chatsworth House, Peak District · Wikimedia Commons
Tue 14 Jul · cooler, weekday
Chatsworth House + Bakewell
⏱ ~1 hr each way

The showstopper. One of England's grandest stately homes in glorious parkland. Do the farmyard, adventure playground and river walk (all pram-friendly) rather than the full house if Alfie's restless — the grounds alone are worth it. Then 15 min to Bakewell for the classic Peak market town and a warm Bakewell pudding. Big walking day, best in the cooler back half of the week.

Chester Zoo · Wikimedia Commons
Wed 15 Jul · weekday
Chester Zoo or Shrewsbury
⏱ Zoo ~1 hr · Shrewsbury ~45 min

Pick your energy level. Chester Zoo is world-class, totally pram-built and an easy full day. Or keep it relaxed with Shrewsbury — the prettiest medieval town nearby, Tudor streets wrapped in a loop of the Severn, plus the big pram-friendly Quarry Park by the river.

Bourton-on-the-Water, Cotswolds · Wikimedia Commons
Thu 16 Jul · flex / buffer
Cotswolds — Bourton-on-the-Water or rest
⏱ ~1 hr 15 each way

Optional last outing, or keep it as slack and packing before the London leg. Bourton-on-the-Water is chocolate-box England — the little River Windrush runs through the green under low stone bridges, paddling-friendly and flat. Arrive by ~10:30 for parking. Add Stow-on-the-Wold 15 min on if you fancy.
