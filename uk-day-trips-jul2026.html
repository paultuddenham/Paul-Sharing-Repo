Sat 11 Jul · sunny, 29°
Cannock Chase — Birches Valley
⏱ ~12 min each way · on the doorstep

The perfect easy opener on a hot day — shady woodland ten minutes from your dad’s, no booking. Birches Valley has flat buggy-friendly trails, the Gruffalo sculpture trail, a forest play area and a good cafe. Low-effort after the BBQ day, and the tree cover keeps Alfie cool while the sun’s at its strongest.

Where to go & options
  • Birches Valley Forest Centre (Rugeley) — the best all-rounder with a toddler: cafe, flat surfaced buggy trails, the Gruffalo trail, forest play area, plus Go Ape & cycle hire for later. Pay & display parking.
  • Sherbrook Valley & the Stepping Stones (from Punch Bowl car park) — prettiest short walk; a carrier beats the pram on the rougher stretches.
  • Wild fallow deer — free-roaming herds on the open heath, best around dawn/dusk.
  • Marquis Drive Visitor Centre (Hednesford) — cafe, small play area, gentle waymarked walks if you want a second base.

Shugborough Hall · Wikimedia Commons
Sun 12 Jul · sunny, 28°
Shugborough Estate + Lichfield
⏱ ~15–20 min each way

A close, gentle day for the weekend. Morning at the National Trust estate — walled garden, riverside parkland, farm animals, all pram-friendly. Then Lichfield’s three-spired medieval cathedral and Beacon Park playground. Shortest drive on the list, so easy to cut short if Alfie’s flagging.

Where to go & options
  • Shugborough (NT): the model farm (animals, old dairy), walled kitchen garden, and easy parkland walks by the River Sow. Free for NT members; mostly buggy-friendly with some gravel. Tearoom on site.
  • Lichfield: the only English cathedral with three spires (step-free access); Beacon Park has a big playground, boating and a summer splash pad; Erasmus Darwin House if you fancy history.
  • With Alfie: pram fine across most of both; combine as a half-day each or lean into whichever he’s happiest at.

Mon 13 Jul · sunny, 25°, breezy
North Wales — Conwy + Llandudno
⏱ ~1 hr 45 each way (the big one)

A proper adventure day, saved for a quiet weekday. Forecast is ideal — sunny, ~25°C with a fresh sea breeze (gusts ~27mph, so a windproof layer on the prom). Conwy is a spectacular walled town with one of Europe’s best-preserved castles on the estuary; then 10 min to Llandudno’s grand Victorian seaside prom (flat, pram-perfect) and the Great Orme tram. Set off early.

Where to go & options
  • Conwy: the castle has steep spiral steps (carrier, not pram, inside); the walkable town walls, the quay, and the Smallest House in Britain are all easy. Good chippy/cafes on the quay.
  • Llandudno: the flat Victorian promenade and pier are pram-perfect; ride the Great Orme Tramway or cable car for big views; sandy-ish beach for a paddle.
  • If you want gardens: Bodnant Garden (NT) is ~20 min inland — buggy-friendly terraces.
  • Logistics: longest drive of the week (~210 mi round). Leave ~9am, pack wind layers and snacks.

Thomas Land, Drayton Manor · Wikimedia Commons
Tue 14 Jul · sunny, 27°
Drayton Manor + Thomas Land
⏱ ~30 min each way (Tamworth)

The big one for Alfie, saved for a quieter weekday. Europe’s only Thomas Land — a whole area of rides scaled for little ones — plus a 15-acre zoo. The single best all-round toddler day. A half-day is plenty at his age. Book online in advance: much cheaper than the gate (roughly £28–34 online vs ~£40 on the day).

Where to go & options
  • Thomas Land: ~25 rides themed around Thomas & Friends, most gentle enough for toddlers (some have a minimum height, so pack the buggy for the in-betweens). Open ~10:30–16:00.
  • Drayton Manor Zoo: 15 acres included with entry — meerkats, monkeys, reptiles and a walk-through aviary; a good cool-down from the rides.
  • With Alfie: go for opening, do Thomas Land first while he’s fresh, then the zoo/lunch after his nap. Pre-book online and screenshot the tickets in case of signal.
  • Hot-day swap: if it’s sweltering, West Midland Safari Park (~45 min) does the drive-through from the air-conditioned car — ideal over a toddler nap.

Chester Zoo · Wikimedia Commons
Wed 15 Jul · sunny, 28°
Chester Zoo or Shrewsbury
⏱ Zoo ~1 hr · Shrewsbury ~45 min

Pick your energy level. Chester Zoo is world-class, totally pram-built and an easy full day — its shaded paths and big indoor houses keep everyone comfortable on a warm one. Or keep it relaxed with Shrewsbury — the prettiest medieval town nearby, Tudor streets in a loop of the Severn, plus the big pram-friendly Quarry Park.

Where to go & options
  • Chester Zoo: aim for The Islands, the indoor Monsoon Forest (warm, dry — your rain backup), the butterfly house and the playground; the land train saves little legs. A full day; pre-book.
  • Shrewsbury alt: Quarry Park & the Dingle gardens are pram-perfect by the river; add the castle and Tudor lanes.
  • Chester city alt: the Roman walls, the two-tier medieval Rows, and a short river cruise.

Thu 16 Jul · sunny, 28°
Chatsworth House + Bakewell
⏱ ~1 hr each way

A grand finale, kept relaxed before Friday’s train to London. One of England’s finest stately homes in glorious parkland — lean into the farmyard, adventure playground and river walk (all pram-friendly) rather than the full house if Alfie’s restless. Then 15 min to Bakewell for the classic Peak market town and a warm Bakewell pudding. Easy to shorten so you’re home in good time to pack.

Where to go & options
  • Chatsworth farmyard & adventure playground: the toddler magnet — animals, a big timber playground and woodland walk; the garden’s cascade and fountains are made for a splash. Go early, it gets busy; pre-book online.
  • The house: worth it if Alfie naps in a carrier — grand painted halls and sculpture gallery.
  • Bakewell: riverside and ducks, the original Bakewell pudding shops, and a pretty market town for a wander.
  • Before London: Friday’s train is 10:49 from Lichfield Trent Valley — an easy Thursday means an unhurried pack that night.
