West Midland Safari Park · Wikimedia Commons
Sat 11 Jul · sunny, 29°
West Midland Safari Park
⏱ ~45 min each way (Bewdley)

The best heat-beater on the list — the 4-mile drive-through safari is done from the air-conditioned car (perfect if Alfie naps), with lions, rhinos, giraffes and more right at the window. Then Land of Living Dinosaurs and a small toddler-friendly theme park. Sun-smart on a hot day and a genuine wow for a little one. Book online to save.

Where to go & options
  • Safari drive-through: ~4 miles, do it slowly with the windows up (they sell animal feed at the gate); the big cats and rhinos are in fenced sections. Loop again if Alfie loves it — it’s all included.
  • Land of Living Dinosaurs & the walk-through animal exhibits for after the drive; a small theme park with gentle toddler rides (some pay-per-ride).
  • Heat plan: do the drive-through mid-afternoon when it’s hottest (you’re in the AC), save the walking bits for morning/late; plenty of shaded picnic spots. Pre-book online and screenshot the tickets.

  • Waterworld, Stoke (~35 min) — indoor tropical waterpark with a toddler zone; the ultimate hot-day escape. Map ↗
  • Twycross Zoo (~35 min) — big zoo plus a large indoor soft-play, so there’s a cool bolt-hole. Map ↗
  • RAF Museum Cosford (~25 min, free) — huge indoor hangars of aircraft, room to roam out of the sun. Map ↗

Conwy Castle · Wikimedia Commons
Sun 12 Jul · seaside, breezy
North Wales — Conwy + Llandudno
⏱ ~1 hr 45 each way (the big one) · with your sister

The seaside day, out with your sister. Conwy is a spectacular walled town with one of Europe’s best-preserved castles on the estuary; then 10 min to Llandudno’s grand Victorian seaside prom (flat, pram-perfect), a sandy beach for a paddle and the Great Orme tram. It’s the longest drive of the week, so set off early — but the coast is the coolest, breeziest place to be on a warm day.

Where to go & options
  • Conwy: the castle has steep spiral steps (carrier, not pram, inside); the walkable town walls, the quay, and the Smallest House in Britain are all easy. Good chippy/cafes on the quay.
  • Llandudno: the flat Victorian promenade and pier are pram-perfect; ride the Great Orme Tramway or cable car for big views; sandy beach for a paddle.
  • If you want gardens: Bodnant Garden (NT) is ~20 min inland — buggy-friendly terraces.
  • Logistics: longest drive of the week (~210 mi round). Leave ~9am; pack sun cream and a wind layer for the prom, plus snacks.
🔄 Alternatives for this day
  • Rhyl & Prestatyn (~1h20, closer coast) — long sandy beaches plus the indoor SC2 waterpark at Rhyl if it’s hot. Map ↗
  • Great Orme, Llandudno — ride the tram or cable car to the summit; the Bronze-Age copper mine is a cool, shaded add-on. Map ↗
  • Bodnant Garden (NT) (~20 min inland from Conwy) — buggy-friendly terraced gardens if you fancy less beach. Map ↗

Winnats Pass, Peak District · Wikimedia Commons
Mon 13 Jul · scenic loop, sunny 27°
Peak District loop — Castleton, Bakewell & Chatsworth
⏱ a full scenic loop (~130 mi round) · drama then shopping

The dramatic drive AND the shopping, in one grand loop. Head up to the High Peak first — the plunge down Winnats Pass, the ridge of Mam Tor and the stone village of Castleton — then wind home south through the dales to Bakewell (Monday is market day) and Chatsworth for its famous Farm Shop and the farmyard/playground for Alfie. A big, driving-heavy day, but scenery and shopping both sorted.

The loop & stops
  • Winnats Pass & Mam Tor: drive the steep limestone gorge slowly (it’s spectacular), then pull in on the Mam Tor ridge for the Hope Valley panorama (paragliders often launch here). Carrier, not pram, for the short steep bits.
  • Castleton: a pretty village for a coffee and ice cream, with the ruined Peveril Castle above; the Blue John / Treak Cliff caverns are a cool option on a hot day (check suitability with a toddler).
  • Bakewell: then south to the classic market town — Monday is market day (9am–4pm, the Peak’s only market), plus independent shops, the riverside and the original Bakewell pudding shops.
  • Chatsworth: finish at the estate — the renowned Farm Shop at Pilsley (craft butcher, on-site bakery, a deli with 104 cheeses, local treats + Peak Ales) and, if there’s time, the farmyard, adventure playground and parkland for Alfie.
  • With Alfie: it’s a long, driving-heavy day (~130 mi) right after Sunday’s Wales run — pick the highlights rather than everything, plan stops around naps, and be ready to trim the caverns or the house if he’s had enough.
🔄 Alternatives for this day
  • Shorten it: skip the High Peak and just do Bakewell + Chatsworth via Ashbourne and the dales (~1h each way) — all the shopping, far less driving. Map ↗
  • Shropshire Hills / Long Mynd (~1h) — a scenic drive over the tops to Carding Mill Valley (NT), a gentle stream walk for Alfie. Closer, softer scenery. Map ↗
  • Buxton (~1h) — elegant spa town: the Victorian Cavendish Arcade shops, Pavilion Gardens (pram-friendly) and the Crescent. Map ↗

Cheshire Oaks Designer Outlet · Wikimedia Commons
Tue 14 Jul · sunny, 27°
Cheshire Oaks Designer Outlet
⏱ ~1 hr 5 each way (Ellesmere Port)

A shopping day at the UK’s biggest designer outlet — ~145 stores at outlet prices (Nike, Polo Ralph Lauren, Boss, Le Creuset, M&S and more) in an open-air "village" layout, flat and fully buggy-friendly with baby-change and plenty of food. An easy, low-stress day with a toddler in tow, and only ~12 min from Chester if you want to add more.

Where to go & options
  • The outlet: ~145 units at roughly 30–60% off; open-air village streets, flat and step-free — easy with a pram. Free parking; grab the centre map/app for the store list.
  • With Alfie: baby-change + feeding rooms on site; lots of casual food (Ed’s Diner, Nando’s, cafes); carrier or buggy both fine.
  • Add-on next door: the Blue Planet Aquarium is right at Cheshire Oaks — a good cool indoor toddler stop if it’s hot.
  • Nearby swap: Chester Zoo is only ~12 min away (world-class, pram-built, shaded + indoor houses) — an easy pivot if the mood is more animals than shopping.

  • Chester Zoo (~12 min) — world-class, pram-built, shaded + indoor houses; an easy pivot to animals. Map ↗

Thomas Land, Drayton Manor · Wikimedia Commons
Wed 15 Jul · sunny, 28°
Drayton Manor + Thomas Land
⏱ ~30 min each way (Tamworth) · last day with the car

The big one for Alfie, on your last full day with the car. Europe’s only Thomas Land — a whole area of rides scaled for little ones — plus a 15-acre zoo. The single best all-round toddler day. A half-day is plenty at his age. Book online in advance: much cheaper than the gate (roughly £28–34 online vs ~£40 on the day).

Where to go & options
  • Thomas Land: ~25 rides themed around Thomas & Friends, most gentle enough for toddlers (some have a minimum height, so pack the buggy for the in-betweens). Open ~10:30–16:00.
  • Drayton Manor Zoo: 15 acres included with entry — meerkats, monkeys, reptiles and a walk-through aviary; a good cool-down from the rides.
  • With Alfie: go for opening, do Thomas Land first while he’s fresh, then the zoo/lunch after his nap. Pre-book online and screenshot the tickets in case of signal.
  • Last car day: the rental goes back tomorrow — it’s an easy ~30-min hop, so aim to be home by late afternoon and pack that evening.
🔄 Alternatives for this day
  • Alton Towers — CBeebies Land (~40 min) — a whole area aimed at toddlers (the rest of the park is thrill rides). Pre-book online. Map ↗
  • Trentham Monkey Forest + Gardens (~35 min) — free-roaming macaques on a woodland walk, plus gardens and a big playground. Map ↗
  • Twycross Zoo (~35 min) — big zoo with indoor soft-play for a hot afternoon. Map ↗

Thu 16 Jul · car back + Birmingham
Return the car + Birmingham shopping
⏱ ~30 min to Birmingham · last day with the car

The rental goes back today. Drive into Birmingham to drop the SIXT car (check your booking for the exact return depot), then a relaxed afternoon shopping in the centre before the train home. Birmingham’s big shopping is all walkable and toddler-friendly, and you’re minutes from the station for an easy hop back to Cannock.

Car return, shopping & the train home
  • Car return: confirm the SIXT drop-off point and time on your booking (pickup was Birmingham Airport / BHX — the return is likely the same). Fuel up nearby first if it’s a full-to-full deal, and photograph the car at handover.
  • Shopping: the Bullring (Selfridges, high-street stores) links straight into Grand Central above New Street station (John Lewis-style mix, food hall). All under cover and step-free — good with a buggy on a hot day.
  • Break spots with Alfie: St Martin’s churchyard green by the Bullring, or the food hall at Grand Central; baby-change facilities in both centres.
  • Train home: frequent services from New Street (or Birmingham International if you dropped the car at the airport) back towards Cannock — a short, easy ride. Then pack for Friday’s 10:49 London train from Lichfield Trent Valley.

  • National SEA LIFE Centre, Brindleyplace (~10 min walk in the centre) — indoor aquarium, very toddler-friendly. Map ↗
