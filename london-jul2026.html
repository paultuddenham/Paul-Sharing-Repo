On the doorstep (all step-free, <10 min)

  • Hay’s Galleria — a covered Victorian glass-roofed arcade ~4 min away with a kinetic water-spouting ship sculpture; the perfect low-stakes wander if the weather turns, with cafes and loos on hand.
  • Early, relaxed dinner (~18:00) from the family dining list below — eat while Alfie’s still cheerful; the casual spots take walk-ins with a pram.
  • If energy is gone, Hay’s Galleria + a room-service night is a perfectly good call.

Sat 18 Jul · family day + shopping
London with dad & sister
⏱ Borough → Covent Garden → Liberty/Carnaby → Camden, ending at Euston

A full family day with dad & sister: breakfast at the Shard, then all together from Borough Market up through Covent Garden and the Carnaby/Liberty shops, finishing in Camden before their 19:38 train home. With the pram, buses & walking beat the tube — and steer clear of the Covent Garden & Camden Town stations (deep / exit-only).

The day, stop by stop
  • ~11:00 · Meet at the Shard — breakfast first at Ting or in-room (no rush). Dad & sister reach London Bridge ~10:50 from Euston, then a 5-min walk to Borough.
  • 11:15 · Borough Market (all together) — graze: Bread Ahead doughnuts, Kappacasein toasted cheese, Monmouth coffee. Quiet POI next door: Southwark Cathedral (free, pram-easy). Pint: The Rake / The Market Porter.
  • 13:00 · Covent Garden (lunch) — easiest with the pram is a black cab from London Bridge (~15 min, ~£13 / S$22); skip Covent Garden tube (deep, not step-free). Lunch: Flat Iron / Dishoom / Shake Shack. The Piazza street performers are a hit with the kids; Neal’s Yard & Seven Dials nearby. Snack: Venchi gelato. Pint: The Lamb & Flag.
  • 14:45 · Liberty + Carnaby — a 12-min walk through Soho (pedestrianised). Liberty (the Tudor landmark), Carnaby St, and Kingly Court (loos, high chairs, Pizza Pilgrims). Pint: BrewDog Soho.
  • 16:45 · Camden — take bus 88 direct from Oxford Circus (~£1.75, step-free, top-deck view); avoid Camden Town tube — exit-only on Sat afternoons & not step-free. Market street food, Camden Lock & the canal; gentler option = the canal walk to Primrose Hill. Pint: BrewDog Camden / The Hawley Arms.
  • ~18:45 · Their train — dad & sister to Euston for 19:38 (arr Lichfield 21:19). From Camden, walk ~15 min or board at Mornington Crescent (Camden Town is exit-only). You & the kids head back to the Shard — daylight till 21:08.

Sun 19 Jul · fly home
Check out + Gatwick (SQ313 12:45)
⏱ Thameslink London Bridge → Gatwick, ~30–35 min, step-free

Light travel morning: relaxed breakfast, check out, and a quick direct Thameslink from London Bridge (right under the hotel) to Gatwick. Aim to be at Gatwick ~2h before the 12:45 flight — so by ~10:45.

Getting to the flight
  • Target a ~10:00 train from London Bridge → leave the Shard ~09:30 with the pram. Trains run ~every 15–30 min (Sunday tends toward every 30).
  • London Bridge is a modern step-free station connected straight to the hotel — no street schlep. Sit toward the rear for the accessible / luggage space.
  • Buy Thameslink tickets ahead or tap contactless (Gatwick is in the zone). With a toddler, banking extra airport slack (the ~09:30 train + a slow coffee at Gatwick) beats a platform dash.
