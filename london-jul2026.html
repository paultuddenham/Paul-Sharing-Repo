Fri 17 Jul · arrive & settle
Arrive + settle at the Shard
⏱ train down · London Bridge connects step-free straight into the hotel

You’ll arrive travel-frayed, so keep it easy: bags down, cot sorted, let Alfie nap, then a flat riverside evening right on the doorstep — nothing that needs a plan.

On the doorstep (all step-free, <10 min)
  • Queen’s Walk / More London riverside — a flat, traffic-free Thames promenade outside the hotel; the More London fountains (jets out of the paving) are toddler catnip on a warm evening (pack a spare top for Alfie).
  • Hay’s Galleria — a covered Victorian glass-roofed arcade ~4 min away with a kinetic water-spouting ship sculpture; the perfect low-stakes wander if the weather turns, with cafes and loos on hand.
  • Early, relaxed dinner (~18:00) from the family dining list below — eat while Alfie’s still cheerful; the casual spots take walk-ins with a pram.
  • If energy is gone, Hay’s Galleria + a room-service night is a perfectly good call.

Sat 18 Jul · South Bank day
South Bank loop
⏱ a pram-friendly riverside mile, ending on the water

A compact riverside loop — Borough Market → Tower Bridge → HMS Belfast → Thames Clipper home — all within a pram-friendly mile, with a boat ride as the finale. Self-paced: bail out or nap-pause anywhere.

Where to go & options
  • Borough Market (go ~10:00) — grazing heaven and fun for a toddler, but it gets very crowded and tight for a pram by midday, so arrive at opening or wear Alfie in a carrier. Step-free; toilets on Borough High St.
  • Tower Bridge — walking across is free, flat and a highlight; the Exhibition is buggy-accessible by lift to the glass-floor walkways (~£12.30 / S$21 adult, under-5s free — check ahead). A good nap-window: Alfie can doze in the pram.
  • HMS Belfast — prams on the open decks only; there’s a pram-park on the Quarter Deck if you want the interior (carrier for below-decks). Baby-change before you board. ~£24.50 / S$42 adult, under-5s free.
  • Thames Clipper (Uber Boat) back to the Shard — board at London Bridge City Pier by HMS Belfast; ramp boarding, pram-friendly, breezy river ride. Cheaper in the app; under-5s free, child fares half price.
  • Rainy/hot alternative: the Natural History Museum (South Kensington) — free, superb for toddlers (dinosaurs, the blue whale), buggies allowed inside, baby-change at the main entrance; arrive ~10:00. NB the Diana Memorial Playground nearby is closed for refurbishment (due to reopen “Summer 2026”, no confirmed date) — check before promising it; the Round Pond / other Kensington Gardens playgrounds are an easy substitute.

Sun 19 Jul · fly home
Check out + Gatwick (SQ313 12:45)
⏱ Thameslink London Bridge → Gatwick, ~30–35 min, step-free

Light travel morning: relaxed breakfast, check out, and a quick direct Thameslink from London Bridge (right under the hotel) to Gatwick. Aim to be at Gatwick ~2h before the 12:45 flight — so by ~10:45.

Getting to the flight
  • Target a ~10:00 train from London Bridge → leave the Shard ~09:30 with the pram. Trains run ~every 15–30 min (Sunday tends toward every 30).
  • London Bridge is a modern step-free station connected straight to the hotel — no street schlep. Sit toward the rear for the accessible / luggage space.
  • Buy Thameslink tickets ahead or tap contactless (Gatwick is in the zone). With a toddler, banking extra airport slack (the ~09:30 train + a slow coffee at Gatwick) beats a platform dash.

Family dining
Baby-friendly dinners near the Shard / Borough
⏱ all casual, high-chair-friendly, toddler-welcome · prices per head (food + a soft drink)

A shortlist of relaxed, family-happy spots within a short pram-stroll of the hotel — nothing fussy. Prices are ballpark; check ahead.

The shortlist
  • Franco Manca (Borough Market) — sourdough pizza; cheap, fast, relaxed, loud enough that a grizzly toddler goes unnoticed. ~£15 / S$26 · map ↗
  • Pizza Pilgrims (London Bridge) — Neapolitan pizza; dough balls to keep little hands busy, high chairs. ~£15 / S$26 · map ↗
  • Wagamama (South Bank / London Bridge) — pan-Asian noodles; bench seating, fast, kids menu, no fuss about mess. ~£18 / S$31 · map ↗
  • The Real Greek (Bankside) — Greek mezze; shareable small plates suit grazing toddlers, easy pram parking. ~£22 / S$38 · map ↗
  • The George Inn (Borough High St) — historic galleried pub (National Trust); hearty food + a big step-free courtyard. ~£20 / S$35 · map ↗
  • Côte Brasserie (London Bridge) — French bistro; smarter but firmly family-friendly, proper kids menu, calm for an early dinner. ~£25 / S$43 · map ↗
  • Borough Market street food — the easiest toddler lunch: pasta / grilled cheese / fruit, eaten by the river. ~£10 / S$17 · map ↗
